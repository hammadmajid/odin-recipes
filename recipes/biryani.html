Biryani

Description

A rich and aromatic rice dish made with basmati rice, marinated chicken, and a blend of spices, typically served with raita or a side salad.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 onions (sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 4 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 inch ginger (minced)
  • 2 green chilies (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 4 tbsp biryani masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 cup fried onions
  • 4 tbsp ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • 1/4 cup warm milk (for saffron)

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, half of the minced garlic and ginger, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Let it sit for 1 hour.
  3. Heat ghee or oil in a large pot and fry the sliced onions until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same pot, add cinnamon sticks, cardamom pods, cloves, and bay leaves. Saut√© for a minute.
  5. Add the remaining minced garlic, ginger, green chilies, and chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  6. Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is browned and cooked through.
  7. Stir in the biryani masala, chopped mint, and coriander leaves. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  8. In a separate pot, boil water with a little salt and cook the soaked rice until it is 70% done. Drain the rice.
  9. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture.
  10. Sprinkle fried onions on top and add saffron strands soaked in warm milk if using.
  11. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes until the rice is fully cooked.
  12. Gently fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot.
